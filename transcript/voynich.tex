\section{Introduction}
In the following, mainly to promote easier readability and comparability, a transcription of the manuscript is provided, based on the transliteration by \citet{takahashi_voynich_2004}.
Takahashi's transliteration is extracted from \citet{schwerdtfeger_voynich_2004} and converted back into glyphs using a font by \citet{bettencourt_voynich_nd}.\footnote{Schwerdtfeger's \textit{Voynich Information Browser} builds on an online archive of Voynich transcriptions that was maintained by Jorge Stolfi but is no longer live (for an archived version of Stolfi's website, see \cite{stolfi_voynich_2005}). Stolfi states to have worked with an ``interlinear compilation of several existing machine transcriptions'' from Gabriel Landini that was edited and made publicly available by Jim Reeds. Presumably, this compilation might have been the result of the \textit{European Voynich Manuscript Transcription} project by Gabriel Landini and Ren√© Zandbergen. For an overview of transcription efforts, see \citet{zandbergen_text_2023}.}

Glyphs that I interpret in another way than Takahashi are printed in \textcolor{orange}{orange}.
Removed spaces are marked with \textcolor{orange}{$\frown$}.
Added spaces are indicated by \textcolor{orange}{$\smile$}.
